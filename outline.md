# Original proposal

## Abstract

Once upon a time, Linux was not popular but it was attractive and intriguing. Today, Linux is everywhere yet often taken for granted. This presentation is one Linux-fan's saga of experimenting with Linux for a few decades, a story inviting all of us to grow a little more Linux-aware and Linux-playful. It is about refusing to quit. And then not being able to. Audience participation will be celebrated.

## Outline

1. Definitions: Operating Systems and Distros (slides with examples)
2. Brief Audience interaction about the joys and perils of operating systems loved and hated
3. My story: from Commodore 64 to Linux in 1999 (slides)
4. Distributions: audience experiences
5. My story: from Redhat to Debian to Arch to Fedora to NixOS...
6. Linux on our coffee maker at work... Weirdest places you have seen it? (Brief audience participation)
8. Hybridized Linux environments (WSL, containers, remote shells...)
9. Conclusion
